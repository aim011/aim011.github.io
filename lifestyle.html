Lifestyle Changes

Here are some lifestyle changes that you could make to become more sustainable.

Reduce CO2 Emissions

  • Buy food locally at farmers markets, this reduces emissions from transporting produce (check out USDA’s Community Supported Agriculture Program)
  • Carpool, walk, bike, or take public transportation when possible

Save Water

  • Wash clothes on low to reduce the amount of rinses
  • Take a shower instead of a bath
  • Take shorter showers
  • Eating less meat, especially red meat, can save water
  • Avoid using AI, opt for a google search or post on a forum to answer a question

Save Energy

  • Dial the A/C up in summer and down in winter
  • Use cold water to wash your clothes
  • Unplug electronics when not in use
  • Use a drying rack instead of a dryer

Save Paper

  • Digitize your bills and coupons, opt out of mail-in
  • Use recycled printer paper and recycled paper notebooks
  • Double-side your print-outs instead of using more paper
  • Buy recycled wrapping paper for the holidays
  • Use rags instead of paper towels for spills and drying hands

Reduce Plastic Waste

  • Carry a reusable water bottle instead of using a plastic bottle
  • Avoid using or buying single use items or items with unnecessary plastic packaging
  • Bag your polyesters when washing them to reduce microplastic shedding, use a bag such as the GUPPYFRIEND bag
  • Bring your own reusable bag to the grocery store
  • Avoid products that contain microbeads, not only are they not good for you, they end up as microplastics in the environment
  • Avoid synthetic and plastic-based clothing, look for wool, linen, silk, and cotton

Reduce Methane Emissions

  • Compost food scraps instead of tossing them; food waste breaks down and emits methane, a harmful greenhouse gas
  • Eat less meat! “Researchers have found that 37% of methane emissions from human activity are the direct result of our livestock and agricultural practices” (EPA)

Reduce Miscellaneous Waste

  • Keep your phone as long as possible
  • When needing to upgrade electronics, opt for a refurbished electronic device
  • Keep your clothes as long as possible and donate instead of trashing old clothes that do not fit
  • Dispose batteries, electronics, and medications with care; the chemicals they contain can harm the environment from landfills (check out your local government’s collection program)
  • Avoid styrofoam cups as they cannot be recycled most of the time

Benefit Wildlife and Animals

  • Do not buy souvenirs made from ivory, tortoiseshell, reptile skin, coral, or exotic fur; even if they are already dead buying it creates a demand for it and more wildlife will be harvested
  • Use vegan and cruelty-free cosmetics
  • Do not kill bees! Trap them in a cup and release them outside, they pollinate the food you eat and the flowers you admire

Connect With Nature

  • Connect with nature, see the earth as a living, breathing being. Dedicate yourself to respecting and loving mother earth
  • Take some time to reconnect with nature, go on a walk, visit a garden, drive out to a national park, etc.
  • If you cannot connect with nature where you are, find a relaxing video and enjoy the beauty of nature from a screen
